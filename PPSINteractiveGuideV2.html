1. The Core Concept: PPS-1

At its heart, the PPS-1 is a fixed daily payment received from **Medicaid** for each day a **Medicaid-eligible client** receives any qualifying service at an IDCC. It functions as a "Daily All-Inclusive Service Fee," meaning a flat rate covers all services provided to a Medicaid-eligible client within a single day, regardless of the number or intensity of those services.

  • Current Daily Rate:
    • $311.31 (with Medicare Economic Index - MEI adjustment)
    • $279.00 (without MEI adjustment)

    The MEI (Medicare Economic Index) is a measure of inflation for the costs of providing physician services. The MEI is an inflation measure for healthcare service costs.

  • "Threshold Visits":

    A "threshold visit" is a qualifying visit where a **Medicaid-eligible client** receives one or more CCBHC services on a given day. Even if multiple services are provided to a Medicaid-eligible client all on the same day, it still counts as just **one threshold visit** for PPS-1 reimbursement. This is because the PPS-1 is a fixed *daily* rate for Medicaid services.

2. How the PPS-1 Rate is Calculated

The PPS-1 rate is a cost-based rate, designed to cover operational expenses. It is fundamentally derived from the following formula:

Total Allowable Annual Costs

=
Total Annual Threshold Visits

(Count of total visits where one or more CCBHC services were provided per 1 day to Medicaid-eligible clients)

This formula highlights a crucial relationship:

  • If **Total Allowable Annual Costs remain stable** and the **Total Annual Threshold Visits increase**, the calculated PPS-1 rate will tend to **decrease**. This means the cost per daily visit becomes lower.
  • Conversely, if **Total Allowable Annual Costs remain stable** and the **Total Annual Threshold Visits decrease**, the calculated PPS-1 rate will tend to **increase**. This means the cost per daily visit becomes higher.
Therefore, effectively managing both costs and ensuring accurate capture of all qualifying threshold visits are vital for the rate calculation.

3. The Critical 3-Year Rebasing Cycle

A crucial aspect of PPS-1 is its **3-year rebasing cycle**. Every three years, the state reviews the actual costs and threshold visit volumes from the previous period to set the new daily rate for the subsequent three years.

Staffing: The Largest Expense

The biggest cost driver is staffing. Fluctuations here, or in other expenses, without corresponding changes in service volume, directly impact the future rate.

Impact on Future Rate

Maintaining stability and consistency in operations and expenses is key to securing a favorable and sustainable PPS rate during each rebasing period. This involves not only managing costs but also ensuring consistent and accurate reporting of all qualifying threshold visits for Medicaid-eligible clients. The state aims for the rebased rate to accurately reflect the actual cost of providing services, meaning efficiency gains (more visits for similar costs) could lead to rate adjustments.

Effective cost management and consistent service delivery (measured by threshold visits) ensure the ability to continue providing essential services while remaining financially healthy.

5. Maintaining CCBHC Status: Beyond Reimbursement

Being a CCBHC is more than just a payment model; it's a commitment to a high standard of care. To keep the PPS-1 rate and operate as a CCBHC, IDCC must consistently meet specific federal and state requirements.

  • Core Compliance:

    Adhering to all rules and standards set for CCBHCs, ensuring IDCC meets its designation requirements.

  • Quality Measure Data:

    Collecting and reporting data on how well services are working and the outcomes for clients.

  • Adherence to Scope of Services:

    Providing all the required types of comprehensive care, from crisis services to peer support, as mandated by the CCBHC model.

**In essence:** Maintaining CCBHC status is about upholding the standards of comprehensive, high-quality behavioral healthcare. It ensures IDCC remains eligible for the PPS-1 rate and positions it as a leader in integrated care.

6. Service Mix & Intensity: Optimizing Operations

While the PPS-1 rate is fixed per daily threshold visit, understanding *what* services are delivered and *how much* effort they take is key to running IDCC efficiently and sustainably.

  • Resource Allocation:

    Matching staff and resources to the actual needs of complex client visits to ensure efficient service delivery.

  • True Cost of Care:

    Knowing if the fixed daily rate truly covers the cost of intensive services provided within a single day.

  • Program Design and Capacity Planning:

    Using patterns in service delivery to improve existing programs, create new ones, and plan clinic capacity effectively.

  • Advocacy:

    Using data to show the real cost of care and advocate for fair reimbursement rates in the future.

**In essence:** Tracking service mix and intensity allows IDCC to manage operations intelligently, ensure sustainable service delivery, and be prepared to advocate for future payment models that might better reflect the complexity of care provided.

7. Process Improvements: Connecting Billing & Finance

Even if billing (using Millin) and finance (using QuickBooks for expenses) seem separate, they **must be closely connected** for accurate financial tracking and effective PPS-1 management. At IDCC, we understand this connection is currently manual.

  • Revenue Visibility:

    Billing generates claims that become IDCC's revenue. Finance needs to see not just *what was billed* but also *what was actually paid* by Medicaid. This helps them track cash flow and true profitability.

  • Expense-Revenue Link:

    Expenses (from QuickBooks) are incurred to provide services that generate revenue (from Millin). Finance needs to compare these to understand if IDCC is financially healthy.

  • Data Flow & Reconciliation:

    Information about claims and payments needs to flow smoothly from Millin to finance for accurate financial reporting and to identify any billing issues. **Currently, this is a manual process at IDCC, which can lead to delays and potential discrepancies.**

**In essence:** Streamlining the connection between billing and finance ensures a complete and accurate financial picture, which is vital for managing the PPS-1 rate and overall IDCC sustainability.

8. Beyond the PPS-1 Rate: Holistic Financial Health

While the PPS-1 rate is a cornerstone of IDCC's funding, it's part of a larger financial ecosystem. Understanding the complete picture is crucial for IDCC's overall sustainability.

  • Other Revenue Streams:

    IDCC likely receives revenue from sources beyond PPS-1 Medicaid, such as Medicare, commercial insurance, grants, or self-pay. These are vital for covering services not eligible for PPS-1 or for clients without Medicaid.

  • Coverage of Non-Billable Services:

    The PPS-1 rate is designed to cover the *total allowable costs* of providing comprehensive CCBHC services, including those that are essential but not individually billable (like care coordination, outreach, and community engagement).

  • Overall Financial Sustainability:

    Combining insights from all revenue streams and expenses provides a true understanding of IDCC's financial health, enabling strategic decisions for long-term growth and mission fulfillment.

**In essence:** A holistic financial view ensures IDCC can effectively manage all aspects of its operations, secure diverse funding, and continue to provide high-quality, comprehensive behavioral healthcare to the community.

Core Compliance Details

This refers to adhering to *all* federal and state certification criteria for CCBHCs. These criteria cover a broad range of operational aspects, ensuring IDCC provides comprehensive and integrated care.

Key compliance areas include:

  • **24/7 Crisis Services:** Availability of mobile crisis teams and crisis stabilization.
  • **Evidence-Based Practices:** Utilization of clinically proven treatment methods.
  • **Care Coordination:** Seamless integration of behavioral health with physical healthcare and social services.
  • **Patient-Centered Treatment Planning:** Individualized plans developed with client and family input.
  • **Staffing Requirements:** Ensuring the presence of a diverse and qualified workforce (e.g., psychiatrists, nurses, peer specialists).
  • **Data Collection & Reporting:** Accurate and timely submission of required operational and quality data.
  • **Accessibility:** Providing services regardless of a client's ability to pay or location.

**Why it's crucial:** Non-compliance can lead to loss of CCBHC designation, which means losing eligibility for the PPS-1 reimbursement model entirely. Regular internal audits and staying updated on regulatory changes are key.

×

Quality Measure Data Details

CCBHCs are required to collect and report on specific quality measures defined by SAMHSA and CMS. These measures track various aspects of care, such as client engagement rates, follow-up after hospitalization, and improvements in symptom severity.

**Why it's crucial:** Beyond compliance, this data demonstrates the effectiveness of services, supports advocacy for funding, and guides internal quality improvement efforts. While not currently tied to direct payments in NYS, it's vital for proving the value of the CCBHC model.

×

Adherence to Scope of Services Details

CCBHCs must provide a comprehensive set of nine core services, either directly or through Designated Collaborating Organizations (DCOs). These include crisis services, screening/assessment, treatment planning, outpatient mental health/SUD services, primary care screening, case management, psychiatric rehabilitation, peer supports, and intensive care for military/veterans.

**Why it's crucial:** Providing this full spectrum of care is a fundamental requirement of the CCBHC model. It ensures clients receive holistic, integrated services and is regularly reviewed as part of the designation maintenance.

Resource Allocation Details

Understanding the mix of services provided within a single daily threshold visit helps managers allocate staff and other resources effectively. For example, a day involving crisis intervention and multiple therapy sessions requires more resources than a day with a single, brief follow-up.

**Why it's crucial:** This insight ensures IDCC has the right staff with the right skills available to meet client needs, preventing burnout or underutilization of resources, even within the fixed daily PPS-1 rate.

True Cost of Care Details

While the PPS-1 rate is fixed, the actual cost to IDCC for a threshold visit can vary significantly based on the intensity and number of services provided. Tracking this allows IDCC to see if the fixed rate truly covers the cost of more complex or intensive daily encounters.

**Why it's crucial:** Identifying services or client needs that consistently cost more than the PPS-1 reimbursement helps in making operational adjustments, seeking efficiencies, or preparing data for future rate negotiations/advocacy.

Program Design and Capacity Planning Details

Analyzing patterns in service mix (e.g., which services are often delivered together on the same day) can reveal insights into client needs and effective integrated care pathways. This data helps in designing new programs or refining existing ones.

**Why it's crucial:** It informs strategic decisions about service offerings, ensures IDCC's capacity (staff, space) aligns with the actual intensity of care provided, and helps optimize client flow.

Advocacy Details

If IDCC consistently provides a high volume of complex, resource-intensive services within the fixed PPS-1 rate, this data can be a powerful tool for advocacy. It demonstrates that the fixed rate, while simplifying billing, might not fully capture the true cost of comprehensive care for all clients.

**Why it's crucial:** This data can be used to support arguments for future rate adjustments, the introduction of value-based payments, or other policy changes that better reflect the complexity and cost of care provided by CCBHCs.

Revenue Visibility Details

Billing systems like Millin are responsible for creating and submitting claims for services rendered. Finance needs to receive accurate and timely information from billing about:

  • **Claims Submitted:** What has been billed and the expected reimbursement.
  • **Payments Received:** Actual cash inflows from payers (Medicaid).
  • **Denials and Adjustments:** Reasons for non-payment or partial payment.

**Why it's crucial:** Without this data, finance cannot accurately forecast cash flow, reconcile bank statements, or determine the true revenue generated by PPS-1 services. This impacts budgeting and overall financial planning.

Expense-Revenue Link Details

The core of financial health for any organization is understanding the relationship between money coming in (revenue from billing) and money going out (expenses from QuickBooks).

**Why it's crucial:** Finance needs to compare total PPS-1 revenue with total allowable costs to assess profitability and ensure IDCC is operating sustainably. This comparison is fundamental for strategic decisions, especially concerning the 3-year PPS-1 rebasing cycle where costs directly influence the future rate.

Data Flow & Reconciliation Details

Even if billing (Millin) and finance (QuickBooks) use separate systems, a consistent and reliable flow of data between them is essential. At IDCC, this process is currently manual.

Challenges of a manual data flow include:

  • **Delays:** Information transfer is not real-time, leading to outdated financial pictures.
  • **Errors:** Manual data entry increases the risk of human error, impacting accuracy.
  • **Lack of Granularity:** Summarized data might hide important details about specific claims or payment issues.
  • **Time-Consuming:** Significant staff time is spent on data entry and reconciliation instead of analysis.

Methods for improving data flow include:

  • **Automated Integrations:** Ideally, direct electronic links between Millin and QuickBooks (or a central data warehouse) to automatically transfer revenue and payment data.
  • **Standardized Reports:** If direct integration isn't possible, establish clear, standardized reports from Millin that finance can easily use for reconciliation and analysis.
  • **Regular Reconciliation Meetings:** Scheduled meetings between billing and finance teams to review reports, identify discrepancies, and resolve issues collaboratively.

**Why it's crucial:** Streamlining this process reduces manual errors, saves time, and provides finance with timely, accurate information needed for strategic decision-making and accurate reporting for PPS-1.
